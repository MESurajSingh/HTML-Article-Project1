Prashanth Neelakantapuram is an Indian film director and screenwriter who primarily works in the Kannada film industry. He made his debut with the 2014 Kannada film, Ugramm and later directed the two-part film series: K.G.F: Chapter 1, and K.G.F: Chapter 2, the latter being the highest-grossing Kannada film.

प्रशांत नीलकांतपुरम एक भारतीय फिल्म निर्देशक और पटकथा लेखक हैं, जो मुख्य रूप से कन्नड़ फिल्म उद्योग में काम करते हैं।

Early life

Prashanth Neelakantapuram[1] was born on 4 June 1980[2] in a Telugu family in Karnataka.[1][3][4] Born to Subhash and Bharathi,[5] their family hails from Neelakantapuram village, near Madakasira, Andhra Pradesh, and settled in Bangalore.[4][5] Neel married Likhitha in 2010. The couple has a daughter and son.[6] Neel's sister is married to Kannada actor Sriimurali, who eventually starred in Neel's directorial debut Ugramm.[7] Neel is also a cousin of Telugu film actor, Aadarsh Balakrishna,[8] and is also related to former Andhra Pradesh state minister, Raghu Veera Reddy.[4]
